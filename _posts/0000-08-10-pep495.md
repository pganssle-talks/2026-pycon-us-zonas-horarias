# PEP 495: Disambiguación de horas locales

<div>

* Introducido en Python 3.6 para resolver el problema de las horas ambiguas.
* Introduce el atributo `fold` en `datetime`.
* Cambios en la comparación de `datetimes` conscientes en horas ambiguas.

</div>

Estar en un lado u otro de la transición es una **propiedad del `datetime`**:


```python
>>> print_tzinfo(datetime(2004, 10, 31, 1, 30, tzinfo=NYC))          # fold=0
2004-10-31 01:30:00-0400
    tzname:   EDT;      UTC Offset:  -4.00h;        DST:      1.0h

>>> print_tzinfo(datetime(2004, 10, 31, 1, 30, fold=1, tzinfo=NYC))
2004-10-31 01:30:00-0500
    tzname:   EST;      UTC Offset:  -5.00h;        DST:      0.0h
```

<br/>

**N.B.:** `fold=1` representa la *segunda* instancia de un `datetime` ambiguo.

Notes:

The solution that they hit on in Python 3.6 was to just add an extra bit of information to the naive portion of the datetime, and that's the `fold` attribute.

So the idea is that you have this extra `fold` attribute and then you can use it to specify which side of an ambiguous transition you want to be on, and then if your `tzinfo` detects that it's in a situation where it needs to make a decision, it can read the `fold` to determine which values to return for those three methods that make up the interface.

And the way it works is that `fold=0` is the *first* instance of an ambiguous time and `fold=1` is the *second* instance.

Notes:

And that's actually a little bit different than the way `pytz` does it, because `pytz`'s parameter is `is_dst` and it's keyed around the DST / STD distinction.

So what they're doing is they're saying that if there's an ambiguous time, you get to select whether you want to be on the DST side or the standard time side. What PEP 495 or the `fold` attribute is doing is it's saying, "Okay, if there's an ambiguous time, do we keep the offset that applied right before the transition, or right after the transition?"

And it turns out this one is a little bit easier to implement, and also it's more well-defined, because as we've learned earlier, sometimes these transitions happen for reasons other than a daylight saving time transition, so you may have standard time on *both* sides or daylight saving time on *both* sides. With fold it's ambiguous because you're just saying, "Are we keeping the values from before the change or the values from after the change?"
